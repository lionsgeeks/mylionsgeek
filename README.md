# MyLionsGeek — Project Overview (Management Report)

> **Purpose of this document:** Explain what MyLionsGeek is, who uses it, and everything it does — written for non-technical readers (leadership, partners, new staff). No technical jargon.

---

## Executive summary

**MyLionsGeek** is the official digital home of **LionsGeek** — the place where students, coaches, administrators, studio managers, and partner companies meet online.

In one sentence: it is an **all-in-one platform** to run LionsGeek day to day — learning, community, physical spaces (studios and coworking), equipment, computers, team projects, job offers, recruitment, attendance, certificates, and real-time communication (messages, stories, calls on mobile).

The public marketing site welcomes visitors and shows how many students and staff are active. After login, each person sees only what their role allows.

---

## What problem does it solve?

LionsGeek trains people in **digital skills** (software development and media creation). Managing that at scale means:

- Tracking who is present and how engaged they are  
- Sharing learning content and homework  
- Booking studios, tables, and meeting rooms  
- Lending computers and audiovisual equipment safely  
- Letting students build a portfolio and a professional profile  
- Connecting graduates with employers  
- Coordinating internal team projects (tasks, files, chat)  
- Keeping the community active (posts, messages, games, rankings)

MyLionsGeek replaces scattered spreadsheets, emails, and separate tools with **one connected experience** on the web, plus a **mobile app** for students and staff on the go.

## Who uses the platform?

| Role | Who they are | Main goal on MyLionsGeek |
|------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Student** | Person accepted into LionsGeek (after the Jungle selection) | Learn, share work, book spaces, apply to jobs, stay in touch with peers |
| **Coach** | Trainer for a class / promotion | Attendance, exercises, quizzes (Geeko), follow student discipline and submissions |
| **Administrator** | Central operations | Users, trainings, analytics, jobs, organisations, moderation, global settings |
| **Moderator** | Support to admin | Users, trainings, jobs, organisations, post reports, equipment (similar to admin on many areas) |
| **Studio manager** | Person responsible for recording studios | Approve studio bookings, verify sessions ended, equipment checks, access requests |
| **Recruiter** | Employer partner (company account) | Post and manage job visibility, browse students, review applications, schedule interviews |
| **Organisation member** | Extra login invited by a company | Help the recruiter account manage the organisation team |
| **Coworker** | User with coworking access | Use shared spaces like students (reservations, feed where allowed) |

A single person can sometimes have **more than one role** (for example coach and admin). The home page after login sends each person to the right area automatically.

---

## How do people get access?

1. **Students** do not self-register. Access is granted after passing the **Jungle** challenge. Credentials arrive by email from the LionsGeek team.  
2. **Staff** accounts are created by administrators.  
3. **Recruiters / companies** are invited by administrators; they complete an **organisation onboarding** (company details, password) before using job tools.  
4. **First login** may require completing the profile (photo, details) via a secure link sent by email.  
5. **Password reset** is available from the login page (email link).  
6. **Email verification** is required for full access on the website.

Support contact mentioned on the public site: **support@lionsgeek.com** and the Help section inside the platform.

---

## Training tracks

Programs are organised into two main paths:

- **Coding** — full-stack development  
- **Media** — media / creative production  

Each **training** (class cohort) has a name, schedule, coach, students, optional exercises, live quiz games (Geeko), and **certificates** (coding or media template) that staff can generate individually or in bulk for the whole class.

## 2. Student experience (web)

### 2.1 Home feed (social wall)

- Scroll posts from the community  
- Create posts with text and images  
- Like, comment, reply, edit or delete own content  
- Repost someone else’s post  
- Report inappropriate posts (goes to staff moderation)  
- Follow and unfollow other members  
- View other members’ profiles and all their posts  

### 2.2 Personal profile & career file

- Profile and cover photos  
- Short biography (“about”)  
- Specialty / field  
- Social links (add, reorder, remove)  
- Work experience entries  
- Education entries  
- Upload and update **CV / resume** (viewable by permitted staff and recruiters)  
- Download **training certificate** when eligible  
- Optional **LinkedIn** connection (settings exist for sharing; certificate share to LinkedIn may be limited to PDF download)

### 2.3 Jobs

- Browse published job offers (full-time, part-time, internship, contract)  
- Filter and read details (skills, location, description)  
- Apply with application stored for recruiters  
- Recruiters and some staff can also open job pages to understand offers

### 2.4 Leaderboard (ranking)

- See rankings of students based on **coding activity time** (connected external time-tracking account per student)  
- Filters: time range (week, month, all time), promotion / cohort  
- Weekly winners and previous week podium  
- Motivates regular practice and visibility of top performers  

### 2.5 Spaces (physical places)

- View **studios** (recording / production rooms) and **coworking tables**  
- See availability and images  
- Start booking flow (subject to permissions: studio access, cowork access flags on the account)

### 2.6 Reservations (my bookings)

- List own studio, cowork, and related bookings  
- Open booking details  
- Cancel own reservations when allowed  
- Receive emails when bookings are approved, declined, rescheduled, or canceled  

### 2.7 Student projects (portfolio)

- List personal projects (title, description, image, files)  
- Submit new projects for **staff approval**  
- Edit pending projects  
- See approval status, rejection reason, and review ratings/notes from staff  
- Approved work is part of the student’s showcase for recruiters  

### 2.8 Exercises (homework)

- View exercises assigned to the student’s training  
- Submit work (files / content per exercise rules)  
- Delete own submission if needed  
- Request a **review** from the coach  
- Get notified when the coach rates or comments  

### 2.9 Geeko (live classroom quiz)

- Join a live session with a **PIN** or link from the coach  
- Wait in lobby until the coach starts  
- Answer timed questions  
- See waiting screen between questions and final results / leaderboard for that session  
- Coach runs the game from the admin side (next question, remove participant, end session)

### 2.10 Games (leisure, often multi-player)

Available games include: Snake, Tic-Tac-Toe, Memory, Tetris, Connect Four, Rock Paper Scissors, Pac-Man, and **UNO** (online lobby with other logged-in users).  
Used for breaks and community building; some games sync state in real time between players.

### 2.11 Chat (private messages — web)

- Message people you follow  
- Conversations list, unread counts  
- Send, read, delete messages; delete whole conversations  
- See a contact’s recent posts from chat  
- Real-time delivery (live updates without refreshing)

### 2.12 Settings

- Edit name, email-related profile fields  
- Change password  
- Delete account (where permitted)

- Create exercises linked to a training

### 4.4 Geeko (quiz builder & live host)

- Create quiz sets with multiple questions and answers

## 8. Cross-cutting features (everyone affected)

### 8.1 Notifications (bell icon & mobile)

Types include:

- Discipline score changes (for coaches/admins)  
- Exercise review requests and ratings  
- New followers  
- Post interactions  
- Project submission and approval status  
- Access requests and responses (studio/cowork)  
- Task assignments and project messages  
- Post report alerts for staff  
- Pending studio reservations (studio manager / admin)  
- Pending appointments  

Users can mark one or all as read. Live updates use a real-time connection while the app is open.

### 8.2 Discipline score (student engagement)

- Calculated from **attendance** over time (missed sessions lower the score)  
- Displayed on student files and training views  
- When the score crosses certain levels, **coaches and leadership get alerts** (and students may get a push on mobile)  
- Used to spot disengagement early  

### 8.3 Search

- Global search for users and relevant items (web and mobile)  

### 8.4 Book an appointment with a person

- Any logged-in user can request a time slot with designated LionsGeek contacts (admins)  
- The recipient gets email with **approve**, **cancel**, or **suggest another time** links (no login required on those email links)  

### 8.5 Certificates

- PDF certificates for coding and media completions  
- Personal download for students; bulk download for staff  
- Public share page with a unique link for proud sharing  

### 8.6 Presence

- While using the site, “last online” updates so others know who is active  

### 8.7 Emails automatically sent by the platform

| Reservation ended / verification | Relevant staff |
| Appointment requested, approved, canceled, time suggested | Both parties |
| Project invitation to join a team project | Invitee |
| New job application | Recruiter / organisation |
| Interview scheduled or outcome | Applicant |

# What MyLionsGeek is **not**

- It is **not** open to the general public for self-registration — membership is controlled by LionsGeek.  
- It is **not** a replacement for external video conferencing for full remote classes (though voice calls exist on mobile between members).  
- Job offers depend on **admin ↔ organisation** setup; not every company on the internet can post freely without invitation.

---

# Business value (why this project matters)

1. **Single source of truth** for students, staff, and partners.  
2. **Visibility** — attendance, discipline, leaderboard, and analytics for decisions.  
3. **Professional outcomes** — portfolios, CVs, jobs, interviews in one loop.  
4. **Asset protection** — studios, equipment, and computers tracked with approvals and end-of-session checks.  
5. **Community & retention** — social feed, chat, stories, games, and rankings keep learners engaged.  
6. **Scalability** — same platform supports multiple promotions, two career tracks, and many partner organisations.
